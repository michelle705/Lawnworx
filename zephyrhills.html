Local Lawn Knowledge

Lawn Care in Zephyrhills Florida

Zephyrhills sits in Pasco County's flat, subtropical zone — an environment that's ideal for warm-season grasses but demands consistent attention. Understanding your grass type and local conditions is the first step to a lawn that stands out on the street.

Grass Types in Zephyrhills: The dominant turf in Zephyrhills is St. Augustine Floratam — a broad-bladed, dark green grass with excellent heat tolerance. Floratam is widely used in new construction homes and HOA communities because it produces dense, attractive turf when properly maintained. The correct mowing height for Floratam is 3.5 to 4 inches. Cutting it too short stresses the plant, invites chinch bugs, and causes yellowing. Bahia grass is also common on older and larger Zephyrhills properties, particularly along Boyette Road and the Crystal Springs area. Bahia is more drought-tolerant and suited to sandy soils, but it grows a distinctive tall seed head that must be removed with frequent mowing. Some properties in Silver Oaks feature Bermuda grass, which performs well in full-sun areas and tolerates a lower mowing height of 1.5 to 2 inches.

Zephyrhills Soil: The soil in Zephyrhills is predominantly sandy — fast-draining, low in organic matter, and naturally acidic with a pH around 5.5 to 6.5. This means nutrients leach out quickly. Without regular fertilization, St. Augustine Floratam will lose its deep green color, thin out, and become vulnerable to dollar weed, sedge, and other invasive weeds that exploit bare soil. LawnWorx recommends 4 to 5 fertilizer applications per year for Zephyrhills lawns.

Watering in Zephyrhills: Irrigation is critical from November through May during Florida's dry season. Floratam needs about one inch of water per week. During the June-through-September rainy season, Zephyrhills typically receives adequate rainfall, but irrigation should continue to fill any gaps. Overwatering encourages gray leaf spot fungus and dollar weed — both common problems in Zephyrhills.

Seasonal Lawn Calendar: In spring (March–May), Floratam comes out of semi-dormancy and growth accelerates — begin weekly mowing by April. Apply a balanced fertilizer with micronutrients in March or April. Summer (June–September) brings peak growth — stick to weekly mowing and watch for chinch bug damage along sidewalks and driveways where the lawn dries first. Fall (October–November) is ideal for a final fertilizer application with potassium to harden the turf before cooler weather. Winter (December–February) is the slow season — bi-weekly mowing is usually sufficient, and over-fertilizing during this period can cause surge growth that weakens the turf.

Common Lawn Pests in Zephyrhills: Chinch bugs are the number one turf pest for Floratam in hot, sunny Zephyrhills yards. They attack the base of grass blades, turning patches yellow and then brown. Sod webworm damage typically appears in late summer as irregular brown areas. Dollar weed and sedge thrive in wet areas of the lawn. Gray leaf spot fungus is a summer threat during hot, humid nights. Regular service and proper turf health are the best preventive measures against all of these.
