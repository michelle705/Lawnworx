Local Lawn Knowledge

Lawn Care in San Antonio Florida

San Antonio FL sits on completely flat, former agricultural and wetland terrain in eastern Pasco County. The Mirada development converted vast stretches of Florida pastureland into a master-planned community with thousands of new homes — virtually all of them seeded or sodded with St. Augustine Floratam as part of the builder package.

Grass in San Antonio FL: St. Augustine Floratam is the dominant grass throughout San Antonio's master-planned communities. It's the ideal grass for this environment — tolerates heat and humidity, produces a dark, attractive turf, and fills in densely enough to crowd out weeds when properly maintained. Builder-installed Floratam on new construction homes needs careful attention in the first year: the root system is still establishing, and improper mowing height or fertilization during this period can set the turf back significantly. New Mirada and Cypress Creek homeowners should begin professional lawn service immediately after closing to ensure proper establishment.

Floratam Mowing Height: The correct mowing height for St. Augustine Floratam in San Antonio FL is 3.5 to 4 inches. Cutting below 3 inches scalps the turf, stresses the plant, weakens root development, and creates openings for weeds, chinch bugs, and disease. LawnWorx maintains the correct height on every visit — we never cut for the sake of cutting; we cut for the health of your turf.

Soil and Nutrients: Sandy Pasco County soil drains fast and loses nutrients quickly. Floratam needs 4 to 5 fertilizer applications annually to stay thick and green. Specific applications include: a balanced fertilizer with micronutrients in spring, a high-nitrogen application in early summer for growth, a balanced midsummer application, and a potassium-heavy fall application to harden turf before the cooler dry season.

Summer Growth in San Antonio: The flat terrain of San Antonio combined with high summer humidity creates conditions where Floratam grows extremely fast — up to 2 to 3 inches per week in peak summer. Without weekly mowing during June through September, lawns grow tall, triggers HOA warnings, and makes the eventual mow more stressful on the turf (never cut more than one-third of blade height at once). Consistent weekly service is not just aesthetic in San Antonio — it's essential for turf health and HOA compliance.

Pests and Disease: Chinch bugs are the primary pest threat for Floratam in sunny San Antonio yards. They cluster near sidewalks and driveways where it's hottest, causing yellowing that spreads inward. Gray leaf spot fungus can appear during hot, humid summer nights. Dollar weed thrives in low, wet areas. Properly dense, well-fertilized Floratam maintained at correct height is naturally resistant to most of these problems.
